# Physical AI Book Documentation Constitution

## Vision
To make complex Physical AI concepts accessible and engaging for beginners to intermediate learners through hands-on Docusaurus-based documentation.

## Core Principles

### I. Hands-On Learning
All documentation MUST prioritize practical examples, interactive code snippets, and guided projects to facilitate active learning. Conceptual explanations MUST be immediately followed by executable examples.

### II. Clarity and Accessibility
Content MUST be written in clear, concise language, avoiding jargon where simpler terms suffice. Visual aids, diagrams, and consistent formatting MUST be used to enhance understanding for beginners and intermediate learners.

### III. Docusaurus-First Design
The documentation structure, navigation, and presentation MUST leverage Docusaurus features to provide an intuitive and efficient user experience. This includes utilizing markdown, MDX, search, and versioning capabilities effectively.

### IV. Community-Driven Iteration
The project will foster an open-source approach, encouraging contributions, feedback, and suggestions from readers and the wider Physical AI community. All major content changes MUST be reviewed and discussed.

### V. Brand Voice
The brand voice MUST be encouraging, knowledgeable, and approachable. It should inspire curiosity and confidence in learners, guiding them through challenging topics with clarity and support.

## Success Criteria
- **Engagement**: High reader retention and interaction with hands-on examples.
- **Clarity**: Positive feedback on the clarity and understandability of complex topics from the target audience.
- **Completeness**: Comprehensive coverage of Physical AI concepts as outlined in the book's roadmap.
- **Maintainability**: Easy to update and extend the documentation using Docusaurus.

## Constraints
- **Target Audience**: Beginners to intermediate learners in Physical AI. All content MUST be tailored to this demographic.
- **Technology Stack**: Docusaurus for all documentation rendering and publishing. Content MUST be compatible with Docusaurus features.
- **Learning Focus**: Strong emphasis on hands-on, practical learning. Theoretical sections MUST always be balanced with practical application.
- **Team Bandwidth**: Limited core team for content creation; MUST rely on clear guidelines for community contributions.

## Stakeholders
- **Authors**: Responsible for core content creation, technical accuracy, and vision.
- **Readers**: The primary beneficiaries; their learning experience and feedback are paramount.
- **Contributors**: Individuals who provide feedback, submit corrections, or propose new content.
- **Docusaurus Community**: Provides the underlying framework and best practices for documentation.

## Governance
This Constitution outlines the foundational principles and guidelines for the Physical AI Book Documentation project. It supersedes all other documentation practices within this project.

**Amendments**: Any proposed amendments to this Constitution MUST be documented, include a clear rationale, and undergo a review process by the core authoring team. Minor clarifications or typo fixes may be applied directly.
**Versioning**: This Constitution will follow semantic versioning.
- **MAJOR**: Backward incompatible changes to core principles, vision, or stakeholder roles.
- **MINOR**: Additions of new principles, sections, or significant expansions of existing guidance.
- **PATCH**: Clarifications, wording improvements, typo fixes, or non-semantic refinements.
**Compliance**: All contributions and content development MUST adhere to the principles and guidelines set forth in this Constitution. Regular reviews will ensure ongoing compliance.
